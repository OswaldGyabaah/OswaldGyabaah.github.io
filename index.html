Projects

Since finding my passion in engineering and technology, I have been working on a few projects outside of class. Most are ongoing projects!

Project Rummie

"The room selection will begin tomorrow at 6am and all participants are expected to assemble at the courtyard or risk missing it". Annually, an email announcing the date of the manual room selection process contains lines similar to the above and goes out to all students in my college.
This is a process in which students are assigned on-campus housing. Each student participates in a lottery that assigns them a unique number which determines their place in the queue to select a room of their choosing, provided it is available. They also have chance to select whoever of is going to be their roommate.
Having gone through this process twice, I saw an opportunity to give back to my university and to simplify this process. In "project Rummie", I design a web app that allow students to go through the entire process without having to physically queue. By design, the process is ten clicks or less and everything else is automated.

The Ashesi Mentorship Project

Imagine the task of having to manually identify the interests of a large group of corporate mentors and pairing them with similar number of students, each pair according to their shared interests. When I was nominated to the team responsible for this task in my school's career department, I thought that was a great opportunity to practice my coding skills in real world an make an actual impact. So, I offered to do this pairing with code for the fist time in the department's history. It worked!!
When the director of the careers sent an email appreciating my work, I thought it was chance to change this process permanently. In a project that is still ongoing, I am converting the code into a web app using flask and python so that anyone can simply do the pairing with a few clicks and have the results communicated automatically to all parties involved. The project is expected to finish by end of 2018.

Humanitarian Engineering Projects

Volunteering with the Engineers Without Boarders, USA, University of Maryland branch, I worked on a humanitarian project in which we built a solar power plant as an alternative to the the national grid which is not stable in that area. As current, we generate about 2.5kW of solar power to the school. This, not only allows the students to study continuously without interruption from power outage, it also helps the school administration cut down cost of electricity as we have deep cycle batteries that store energy for future use.
In a followup project, we built a 20,000 liter water storage unit to support the student during water shortages.

Autonomous Irrigation

In my first adventure in engineering and technology, I designed, built and programmed a solar powered irrigation system together with two of my classmates. We automated its functionality by using sensors and the and arduino hardware, to programmatically check the amount of moisture in the soil. Having researched the optimum moisture for our plants, we programmatically turn on an electric water pump that runs water through the nozzles of a sprinkler to water the plants.
Also, we use a water flow meter to keep track of how much water has been used at any time and compare that with the readings from the soil moisture sensor to determine when the water is enough.
